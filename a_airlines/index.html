American Airlines Audit


Role: UX/UI Designer
Tools: Figma, Canva
Timeline: In progress (Fall 2023)

Task

To heuristically evaluate and develop design recommendations for one flow of a web or mobile UI

Context

American airlines stands as one of the foremost airline companies in operation today. With no age-related restrictions, anyone aged 18 and above can easily book flights, making their services accessible to a wide range of individuals.

While the "poor design choices" in this application may not be readily apparent, I selected it because it's a tool I frequently use, and it appeared to be a stimulating challenge for me to envision how it could be enhanced.

Design Research

I focused primarily one flow for this company. I chose the "Booking a flight" flow as I believe it is the most frequently used by all users of this application.

Heuristic Evaluation

I started off by auditing the mobile app using Nielson Norman's Ten Usability Heuristics. During this, I discovered there were a lot of steps involved in the process of booking a flight and there was no visible way to know how far out the user is. I also noticed that users are not always in control during this process.

Competitive Analysis

I compared it to other airline booking systems and observed that when listing different flight tiers, other websites have a single page that provides a quick overview for users to make decisions swiftly. In contrast, American Airlines requires several steps to access the same information. This is ilustrated below.

Design

I chose option 2 because of its compact design, which is less distracting. An added benefit is that it somewhat resembles an airplane, which aligns with our theme.
Next, I delved into color choices for this component. Option 1 represents one of the official colors for our brand. However, it fails to deliver a good contrast ratio. While considering a shade of green, which symbolizes the official color of good progress, I ultimately settled on option 3. This choice allows us to maintain our brand identity while preserving a minimalist approach and a good contrast ratio.
